Help and advice

Whether you want advice on understanding how you are feeling, get support or information on mental health our team is happy to help. If you need to speak to someone be sure to reach out and one of our experinced team members will be there to help.

Here are a few self-help things you can do to improve your mental health:

  • Talk with friends or family
  • Exercise - it can simply be just going for a walk
  • Take up a hobby - this can be painting, drawing, learning a language etc.
  • Try journelling - simply getting your thoughts and feelings out is better than keeping them locked in!
Remember things will get better!

If you are at immediate risk or harm, please call 999 and ask for an ambulance.

Supporting your young person

Whether your a parent or a guardian, its understandable to be worried about your child or young person and not know what to do to help them. It can be hard to know how, when and where to get support. If your young persons mood or behaviour changes from what it 'usually' is and you are unsure why, this is the place to get some information and advice.

Here are a few things you can do:

  • Check in with them daily
  • Encourage them to get a hobby or do somehting the enjoy
  • Listen and encourage them
  • Try other methods if they dont want to talk such as texting, writing notes
We know looking after someone is hard and it can be even more difficult if your young person is struggling with their mental health but be sure to have patience and let them know you will be there for them and want to listen.

Free group sessions

We are now offering free youth-led group sessions to talk, listen and interact with your peers to see that you are not alone. These sessions are led by our young person volunteers who have had a battle with mental health and overcome. Here you can talk about any feelings you may have, ask any questions or simply listen. We also offer actvities during these sessions to help better understand your emotions.
